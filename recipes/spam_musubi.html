Spam Musubi

So Simple, Yet So Delicious!

Hungry, but not in the mood to spend countless hours prepping? Look no further!

Spam Musubi, originating from Hawaii, is a simple dish made from spam, rice, seaweed, and a few common sauces. Follow this recipe, and within 30 minutes you will have a wonderful dish - It's the perfect snack or meal!

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces of spam
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • Roasted seaweed
  • 6 cups of cooked rice

Steps

  1. In a bag, mix together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar until dissolved.
  2. Slice the spam into 6-10 slices, and add it to the bag. Marinate for roughly 15 minutes.
  3. Remove the spam from the bag, and fry the spam in a skillet over medium heat.
  4. While the spam cooks, place the seaweed on a cutting board. With the seaweed on the bottom, mold rice over the seaweed so that it is roughly an inch thick.
  5. Add a sliced cook of spam on top of the rice. Fold the seaweed over the top. Add water to seal the seaweed if needed.
